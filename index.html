Sir Alexander Fleming

The man who changed the modern medicine

Here is Alexander Fleming's life timeline

Date Event
August 6th, 1881 Alexander Fleming is born at Lochfield farm near Darvel in Ayrshire, Scotland. He is the third of four children born to farmer Hugh and Grace Sirling Monton. Hugh also had four children from his first marriage.
1900-1914 Service in a military.Served as a Private in the London Scottish Regiment
1903 Fleming enrolls in St. Mary's Hospital Medical School in Paddington.
1906 After graduating with distinction, Fleming joins the research department at St. Mary's, where he becomes an assistant bacteriologist to Sir Almroth Wright a pioneer in vaccine therapy and immunology.
1908 Fleming earns a Bachelor of Science degree in Bacteriology. He serves as a lecturer at St. Mary's until 1914.
December 24th,1915 Fleming married a trained nurse, Sarah Marion McElroy of Killala, County Mayo, Ireland.
1916-1918 Fleming serves as Captain of the Army Medical Corps during World War I, spending most of his time in battlefield hospitals.
1921 Fleming discovers an important bacteriolytic substance he names Lysozyme.
1928 Fleming is elected Professor of Bacteriology at the University of London.
September 28th,1928 Fleming discovers the world's first antibiotic after mold he accidentally develops creates a bacteria-free circle around itself.
March 7th,1929 Fleming names his finding "penicillin." Later that year, Fleming publishes his findings in the British Journal of Experimental Pathology. His findings are largely ignored.
1940 Howard Florey and Ernst Borio Chain at the Radcliffe Infirmary in Oxford continue Fleming's research and develop a way for mass producing it.They will begin mass producing it in 1941.
1943 Fleming is elected Fellow of the Royal Society and knighted in 1944.
1945 Along with Florey and Chain, Fleming is awarded the Nobel Prize in Medicine.
1949 Fleming's wife Sarah dies. The couple had only one son, Robert, who went on to become a General Medical Practitioner.Fleming will marry Dr. Amalia Koutsouri-Vourekas, a Greek colleague, in 1953. Shedies in 1986.
March 11th,1955 Fleming dies of a heart attack in his London home. He is buried at St. Paul's Cathedral.
